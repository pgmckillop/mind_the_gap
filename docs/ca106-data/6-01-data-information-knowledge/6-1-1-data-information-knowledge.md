## 6.1.1 Data, information and knowledge

---

## Definition and Purpose
Data, information, and knowledge represent **different stages in the use of facts and meaning** within digital software development.  
Understanding the differences and relationships between them supports effective analysis, decision-making, and communication.

---

## Data
- **Data** consists of raw, unprocessed facts or measurements
- Data has **no inherent meaning**
- It is collected directly from sources

**Characteristics of data:**
- unorganised
- context-free
- requires processing to become useful

---

## Information
- **Information** is data that has been **processed, organised, or structured**
- Processing gives data **context and meaning**
- Information supports understanding and interpretation

**Characteristics of information:**
- structured or formatted
- meaningful within a context
- derived from data

---

## Knowledge
- **Knowledge** is information that has been **interpreted and understood**
- It is formed through experience, learning, or analysis
- Knowledge supports judgement and informed action

**Characteristics of knowledge:**
- based on understanding
- used to make decisions
- derived from information

---

## Relationship Between Data, Information and Knowledge
- There is a **progressive relationship**:
  - data is collected
  - data is processed into information
  - information is interpreted to form knowledge
- Each stage depends on the **quality and suitability** of the previous stage
- Poor-quality data limits the value of information and knowledge

---

## Exam Focus
You should be able to:
- Define data, information, and knowledge
- Distinguish clearly between the three terms
- Explain how data becomes information and then knowledge

!!! tip "Exam tip"
Marks are awarded for showing the **relationship between the three stages**, not just giving separate definitions.
