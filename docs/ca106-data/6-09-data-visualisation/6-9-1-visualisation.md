## 6.9.1 Data visualisation

### Data visualisation formats and their use

**Data visualisation** presents data in visual or structured forms to support understanding, analysis and communication.

### Graphs
- **Description**: Visual representations showing relationships between variables.
- **When used**: To identify trends, patterns or changes in data over time or across values.

### Charts
- **Description**: Visual summaries of data using shapes such as bars or segments.
- **When used**: To compare values or show proportions within a dataset.

### Tables
- **Description**: Data arranged in rows and columns.
- **When used**: To present precise values and allow detailed comparison.

### Reports
- **Description**: Structured documents combining data, explanations and conclusions.
- **When used**: To communicate findings, analysis and outcomes to stakeholders.

### Dashboards
- **Description**: Visual interfaces displaying key data and metrics.
- **When used**: To provide an overview of performance or status, often in real time.

### Infographics
- **Description**: Visual representations combining data and graphics.
- **When used**: To communicate information clearly to a broad or non-technical audience.

Different formats are selected based on the purpose of communication and the needs of the audience.

!!! tip "Exam tip"
In exam answers, link each visualisation format to **its purpose and audience**. Marks are awarded for explaining *why a format is suitable*, not just for naming it.
