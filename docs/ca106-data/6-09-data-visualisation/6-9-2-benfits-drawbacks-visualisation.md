## 6.9.2 Benefits and drawbacks of data visualisation formats

### Considerations based on data type, audience and brief

The suitability of **data visualisation formats** depends on the **type of data**, the **intended audience** and the **brief**.

### Type of data
- **Benefits**:
  - Visual formats can highlight patterns, trends and comparisons in data.
  - Structured data can be clearly represented using tables, charts or graphs.
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Complex or unstructured data may be oversimplified.
  - Inappropriate formats can obscure important details or accuracy.

### Intended audience
- **Benefits**:
  - Visualisations can improve understanding for non-technical audiences.
  - Dashboards and infographics support quick interpretation.
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Highly detailed formats may overwhelm non-technical users.
  - Over-simplified visuals may lack detail for technical audiences.

### Brief
- **Benefits**:
  - Choosing formats aligned to the brief ensures relevant communication.
  - Dashboards and reports can focus on key metrics and outcomes.
- **Drawbacks**:
  - Misalignment with the brief can result in irrelevant or misleading visuals.
  - Overuse of visuals may distract from required information.

Effective data visualisation balances clarity, accuracy and relevance.

!!! tip "Exam tip"
For full marks, explicitly link **one visualisation format** to **data type, audience and brief**, explaining both a benefit and a drawback. Examiners reward balanced, contextualised answers.
