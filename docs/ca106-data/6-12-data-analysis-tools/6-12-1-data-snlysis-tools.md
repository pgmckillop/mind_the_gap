## 6.12.1 Data analysis tools

### Data analysis tools, their purpose and use

**Data analysis tools** are used to store, analyse and derive insight from data to support decision-making in digital software development.

### Storing Big Data for analysis

- **Data warehouse**:
  - **Purpose**: To store large volumes of structured data in a central location.
  - **When used**: For analysis of historical data to support reporting and decision-making.

- **Data lake**:
  - **Purpose**: To store large volumes of raw data in its native format.
  - **When used**: Where data is unstructured or requires flexible analysis.

- **Data mart**:
  - **Purpose**: To store a subset of data focused on a specific business area.
  - **When used**: For targeted analysis by departments or teams.

!!! tip "Exam tip"
Examiners expect you to distinguish clearly between **raw data storage** and **structured analytical storage**.

---

### Analysis of data

- **Data mining**:
  - **Purpose**: To identify patterns, trends and relationships within data.
  - **When used**: To extract insights from large or complex datasets.

- **Reporting**:
  - **Purpose**: To present analysed data in a structured format.
  - **When used**: To communicate findings to stakeholders.

!!! tip "Exam tip"
In exam answers, explain *what insight is gained* from data mining or reporting, not just that they analyse data.

---

### Use of business intelligence gained through analysis

- **Financial planning and analysis**:
  - **Purpose**: To support budgeting, forecasting and financial decision-making.
  - **When used**: To assess performance and plan future activity.

- **Customer Relationship Management (CRM)**:
  - **Customer data analytics**:
    - **Purpose**: To analyse customer behaviour and preferences.
    - **When used**: To improve products, services and engagement.
  - **Communications**:
    - **Purpose**: To manage and personalise interactions with customers.
    - **When used**: To improve customer retention and satisfaction.

Business intelligence enables organisations to make informed, data-driven decisions.

!!! tip "Exam tip"
For higher marks, link **the analysis tool** to **the business decision it supports**. Examiners reward answers that show clear purpose and context.
