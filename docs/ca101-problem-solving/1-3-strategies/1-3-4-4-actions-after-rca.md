# 1.3.4 Actions After Root Cause Analysis

---

## Purpose of Post-Analysis Actions
After completing **root cause analysis**, appropriate actions must be taken to ensure:
- The issue is properly resolved
- The solution is recorded
- Similar issues are prevented in future
- Responsibility and accountability are clear

These actions support **continuous improvement**.

---

## Key Actions

### Logging the Issue
- Record:
  - the problem
  - the root cause
  - actions taken
- Creates an audit trail
- Supports organisational learning

**Why this matters**
- Helps identify recurring issues
- Supports future troubleshooting

---

### Closing the Issue
- The issue can be closed when:
  - the root cause has been addressed
  - the solution has been implemented
  - the problem no longer occurs

**Why this matters**
- Confirms resolution
- Prevents unnecessary rework

---

### Escalating the Issue
- Escalate if:
  - the issue cannot be resolved at the current level
  - specialist knowledge is required
  - the risk or impact is high

Escalation may involve:
- senior technical staff
- management
- external support

**Why this matters**
- Ensures serious issues are handled appropriately
- Reduces risk to systems and users

---

## Choosing the Correct Action
The action taken depends on:
- Severity of the issue
- Impact on users or systems
- Likelihood of recurrence
- Available skills and authority

---

## Exam Focus
You should be able to:
- Describe actions taken after root cause analysis
- Explain why logging, closing, or escalating is appropriate
- Justify the chosen action in a given scenario

!!! tip "Exam tip"
Questions often reward answers that **link the action taken to risk, impact, and accountability**.
